For Prospective Members

Joining Our Community

We welcome individuals who resonate with our values and are called to a life of spiritual pilgrimage, creative expression, and intentional community. Membership involves a thoughtful process to ensure mutual alignment.

The Path to Membership

  1. Learn About Us: Review our About page and documentation thoroughly
  2. Initial Contact: Reach out expressing your interest and background
  3. Visit or Attend: Join us at a gathering or when we're in your region
  4. Trial Period: Participate in community life to experience our lifestyle
  5. Formal Application: Submit application with community references
  6. Consensus Decision: Full assembly discusses and decides on membership

What We Look For

  • Alignment with our spiritual and ethical values
  • Commitment to mobile, traveling lifestyle
  • Skills or willingness to develop a business/craft
  • Openness to poly-pansexual governance structures
  • Dedication to collective well-being and children
  • Respect for Buddha Maitreya's visionary authority

For Communities

Invite Us to Your Region

We're interested in visiting diverse communities worldwide as part of our sacred pilgrimage. Communities can invite us for extended stays or brief visits.

What We Offer

  • Performances: Music, dance, theater, and creative arts
  • Workshops: Skills sharing in various crafts and practices
  • Spiritual Practices: Meditation, ceremonies, and teachings
  • Cultural Exchange: Sharing stories and learning from your traditions
  • Service Projects: Contributing to community needs
  • Economic Activity: Local spending and craft sales

What We Need

  • Accommodation for our group (camping, shared spaces, etc.)
  • Access to basic facilities (water, sanitation, cooking)
  • Venues for events and activities
  • Local orientation and community connections
  • Support navigating regulations and logistics

Interfaith Dialogue

As a Buddhist-rooted community with interfaith openness, we welcome spiritual exchange with communities of all traditions. We honor the sacred in all its forms and seek mutual understanding and respect.
